Pinoy Pork Humba Recipe

Introduction

Pinoy Pork Humba is a Filipino classic that features tender, braised pork belly in a savory-sweet sauce. The rich flavors of soy sauce, vinegar, and a touch of sweetness make this dish irresistible. Perfect for family gatherings or special occasions!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup dried mushrooms, soaked and sliced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a bit of cooking oil over medium heat. Brown the pork belly chunks on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, saut√© onions and garlic until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the pork belly back to the pot. Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, and water. Stir in the brown sugar, bay leaves, and peppercorns.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally and add more water if necessary.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If using dried mushrooms, add them in the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice. Share and enjoy!
