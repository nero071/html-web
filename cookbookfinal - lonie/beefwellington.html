Beef Wellington Recipe

Introduction

Beef Wellington is an elegant dish that features a perfectly cooked beef tenderloin coated with mushroom duxelles, wrapped in prosciutto, and encased in flaky puff pastry. It's a show-stopping main course for special occasions and holiday feasts.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef tenderloin, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 12 oz mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup shallots, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup fresh thyme leaves
  • 8 slices prosciutto
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400째F (200째C).
  2. Season the beef tenderloin with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until browned. Remove from heat and brush with Dijon mustard. Let cool.
  3. In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add shallots and garlic and cook until softened. Add mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become golden brown. Pour in the white wine and cook until evaporated. Stir in heavy cream and thyme. Let cool.
  4. Lay out the prosciutto slices on a sheet of plastic wrap, slightly overlapping. Spread the mushroom mixture evenly over the prosciutto. Place the beef tenderloin on top and roll up tightly using the plastic wrap. Chill in the refrigerator for 15 minutes.
  5. Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface. Unwrap the beef from the plastic wrap and place it in the center of the pastry. Fold the pastry over the beef, sealing the edges with the beaten egg. Place seam-side down on a baking sheet.
  6. Brush the top of the pastry with the beaten egg. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the beef reaches an internal temperature of 125째F (52째C) for medium-rare. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  7. Serve with your favorite sides and enjoy!
